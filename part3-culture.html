Part 3 — Football culture, crowds, and discipline

The external context: who shows up to watch, how aggressively the game is officiated, and who actually gets picked to manage a winner.

Viz 9 — The stadium boom

Average match attendance across every World Cup. The USA 1994 spike still stands as the highest-attended World Cup ever — a product of hosting matches inside enormous American football stadiums built for a completely different sport.

Stadium capacity data exists in this source but isn't cleanly linkable to individual match attendance figures (the attendance records use free-text stadium names rather than a shared ID), so this tracks raw average attendance rather than a stadium-fill percentage.

Viz 10 — The aggression index

Total yellow and red cards per match, by tournament. Cards weren't tracked before 1970 — the card system itself was invented for that World Cup. 2006 stands as the most heavily-carded tournament ever, the same edition as Portugal vs Netherlands' infamous four-red-card "Battle of Nuremberg."

Viz 11 — Native manager or foreign import?

Average furthest stage reached, comparing teams managed by one of their own nationals against teams managed by a foreigner. Click either bar to break it down by how many prior World Cups that manager had already worked.

This dataset has no manager birthdate, so age can't be analyzed — only nationality and prior tournament experience. "Furthest stage" uses the same group stage (1) through final (6) / champion (7) scale as the rest of this project.

Viz 12 — How old are winning managers?

Age of the manager at the time they won the World Cup, for all 22 title-winning instances. Since this dataset itself doesn't include manager birthdates, these ages were researched individually from each manager's public biography rather than pulled from the source data directly.

Alberto Suppici remains the youngest ever at 31 (Uruguay, 1930); Vicente del Bosque the oldest at 59 (Spain, 2010). Ages are approximate to within about a year in a few early cases where an exact birth date was harder to confirm.

Attendance and discipline data derive from the Fjelstul World Cup database and its derived summary tables. Manager ages were researched individually from public biographical sources, since this dataset has no manager birthdate field.
